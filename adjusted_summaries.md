# Adjusted Chapter Summaries — SHINRYU

> Process: For each chapter, the original beat structure is reproduced, a
> five-point conflict check is run against `src/lore.md`, and any conflicting
> beats are adjusted. Conflict-check axes: (1) Leverage awareness, (2) Exile
> residue, (3) Reunion calibration, (4) Setting fidelity, (5) Supporting cast
> consistency.

## Ch 1: Before the Gate

**POV:** Isabelle (Sections I–III), Caspar (Section IV)

**Summary:** Isabelle wakes in her apartment on the morning of her first day at
Sakuragaoka High. She notices the Darmstadt toaster—a Helena Waldstein
import—and spreads Viennese apricot jam on rye toast while Auntie Choi oversees
a full Korean breakfast. Isabelle studies herself in the mirror: five-ten,
angular from the Seoul year, luminous skin from the Waldstein Botanicals
regimen, honey-blonde hair she decided not to cut because Caspar used to look at
it like a color he wanted to photograph. She packs three skincare bottles into
her school bag, clips on her ID (Class 1-3, Shin Ji-won), and walks to school.
The apartment is comfortable but modest—a standard 2LDK in Setagaya, with a
small kitchen where Auntie Choi works with militant precision. At Sakuragaoka,
she enters through the shoe-locker area, where she sees Caspar for the first
time since the 2.5-year exile. The recognition is instantaneous and physically
destabilizing. She passes him in the hall with twelve meters between their desks
and begins the first day of the life they were blackmailed into sharing.
Caspar's section covers his arrival: Class 1-2, his deliberate invisibility, and
his awareness that Isabelle is across the hallway.

**Lore adjustments:**

- **Setting fidelity (apartment location):** The soft version places Isabelle in
  "Minami-Aoyama" with "floor-to-ceiling windows." Lore specifies "modest
  Setagaya apartments" with "small balconies and narrow kitchens." Adjusted: the
  apartment is in Setagaya Ward, a standard 2LDK. No floor-to-ceiling windows.
  The Darmstadt toaster remains (family import), but the apartment itself is
  unassuming—consistent with the parents' mandate that they live humbly.
- **Setting fidelity (school name):** The soft version calls the school
  "Sakuragaoka High." Lore specifies "Tokyo Metropolitan Setagaya Sogo High
  School (Seta-sō)." Adjusted: all references to "Sakuragaoka" become "Seta-sō"
  or "Setagaya Sogo."
- **Exile residue (exile duration framing):** The soft version references "the
  year in Seoul" and "an entire year." Lore specifies a 2.5-year exile
  (2015–2018). The Seoul training was one portion of that exile (~10 months).
  Adjusted: mirror-scene internal monologue references "the exile" or "the two
  and a half years," with the Seoul training as one chapter within it. The face
  changes, the skin regimen, the trainee discipline—all are products of the full
  exile, not just the Seoul stint.
- **Reunion calibration:** The soft version's first sighting is charged but
  understated. Adjusted: deepen the interiority. These are two people who
  haven't touched in 2.5 years, whose proximity is engineered by the same
  parents who punished them. The twelve meters should carry the weight of
  blackmail, betrayal, and longing compressed into silence.
- **Leverage awareness:** No explicit leverage interiority appears in the soft
  version's first morning. Adjusted: add a micro-beat where Isabelle registers
  the cost of being here—VESPER's debut in exchange for compliance—in the moment
  she clips on her school ID or steps out the door.

**Flags for drafting:**

- The Darmstadt toaster is a perfect Helena artifact—keep it, but the kitchen
  around it should be small, Setagaya-standard, not luxury.
- Korean breakfast scene: Auntie Choi's cooking canon (kimchi-jjigae, banchan)
  is lore-consistent. Anchor the sensory density here.
- The mirror scene should reference 2.5 years of exile transformation, not just
  "the year in Seoul."
- The shoe-locker moment is the chapter's emotional hinge: reunion after exile,
  in a public space, under leverage.
