## 9. SUMMARY ASSESSMENT

**Fidelity:** High. Every major lore element is represented. No core character
trait, relationship, or plot mechanism was discarded.

**Changes:** Two minor prop substitutions (oven, car). Both are arguably
improvements for the Japanese setting.

**Expansions:** The chapters are significantly richer than the lore. The lore
provides a skeleton; the chapters add:

- Emotional architecture (the kiss, the year of silence, the "not yet" echo)
- Sensory detail (food, photography, skincare, darkroom chemistry)
- Secondary characters (the Aunties, the VESPER members, the hotel staff)
- Narrative devices (the language filter, the photographs as a love language,
  the Korean/Korean divide)

**Pacing:** The lore's "absolute united front" is the destination. Chapters 1–7
cover approximately two weeks and end with the first sustained physical contact
(hand on chest) and a mutual "not yet." The story is roughly at the midpoint of
the romantic arc described in the lore.

**Risk areas:**

- The unused lore elements (part-time jobs, extended family, language exchange)
  need to be woven in if the story continues beyond chapter 7.
- The VESPER secret is being set up as a major reveal for both Sora (Isabelle's
  past) and potentially the whole friend group (Caspar's fandom). The chapters
  are handling this carefully but it needs a payoff.
- The parents' manipulation is known to the reader and the Aunties but not to
  the friends or (fully) to the protagonists. This creates dramatic tension that
  will need resolution.
