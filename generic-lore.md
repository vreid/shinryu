# Story Bible Summary

## Core Premise

Two teenagers from wealthy European-Korean families arrive at a Japanese high
school in their second year. They're deeply in love, battle-tested by three
years of separation, and completely unaware that their level of intimacy is
shocking to everyone around them. European "old married couple" energy collides
with Japanese high school social norms.

**Tone:** Comedy-forward with serious emotional undertones

**Format:** Episodic web novel with each chapter exploring a specific concept or
situation

---

## Main Characters

### The Girl

- Born 2001
- Father: Dutch (old European money)
- Mother: Korean (CEO of one of the biggest K-pop agencies in Korea)
- Secondment (2013/14-2016/17): K-pop trainee in her own mother's industry
- Sent into the grinder as a nobody not a princess
- Mother's message: "You want independence? Earn it in my world"

### The Boy

- Born 2001
- Father: Korean (chaebol)
- Mother: German (old European money, cosmetics industry)
- Secondment (2013/14-2016/17): Farm hand in Jeju
- Possible connection: Jeju is famous for natural cosmetic ingredients
  potentially tying to his mother's supply chain
- Has rough hands that don't match his wealthy background

---

## Shared Backstory

### The Swiss Years (Until age 13)

- Both attended elite Swiss private school
- Received exceptional education including:
  - Advanced math and sciences
  - Multilingual instruction (French, German, English minimum)
  - IB Middle Years Programme or equivalent
  - Critical thinking and study discipline
- Both families planned a corporate merger through arranged marriage at 25
- Kids figured out the plan at 13
- Rebellion triggered punishment secondments

### The Separation (2013/14 - 2016/17)

- Three years apart in drastically different environments
- Maintained **secret digital contact** throughout
  - Texts and calls during exhausting days
  - Became each other's emotional lifeline
  - Built a private world parents couldn't control
- Bonded over shared punishment creating an unbreakable unit

### The Parents' Miscalculation

- Separation meant to cool things down
- Instead created a shared enemy (the families)
- Parents wanted slow controlled bonding they could manage
- Kids arrived at the other end deeply in love and battle-tested

## Academic Context

### Why They Can Function in Japanese High School

- Japanese known from early age (conversational fluency)
- Swiss elite education provides strong foundations through 7th/8th grade
- Smart enough to reactivate knowledge rather than build from scratch
- Learning how to learn skills from elite schooling

### Where They Still Struggle

- **Classical Japanese (古文)** and **Chinese classics (漢文)**
- Three-year gaps in sequential subjects
- Academic Japanese vs conversational Japanese
- Girl has fragmented education from trainee schedule
- Boy has been out of formal education entirely

### Coping Mechanisms

- Juku (cram school) alongside regular classes
- Possible sympathetic teacher advocate
- Private school with flexible admissions
- They visibly struggle in some subjects while excelling in others

## The Comedy Engine

### The Central Joke

Every normal Japanese high school romance norm gets obliterated by their casual
deep intimacy

**Japanese teen romance expectations:**

- Shy confessions behind the school building
- Nervously walking home together
- First name usage being a huge deal
- Hand holding being peak PDA

**What these two do instead:**

- Pick lint off each other's uniforms
- Share food from the same chopsticks
- Casual forehead touches when talking
- Fix each other's collars naturally
- Finish each other's sentences
- Bicker about nothing then immediately be fine
- She steals his jacket without asking
- He automatically carries her bag
- Pet names in three different languages
- They genuinely don't understand why any of this is unusual

### Cultural Collision Comedy

- Dutch directness + German bluntness vs Japanese politeness norms
- They say exactly what they think and classmates are horrified and fascinated
- Learning tatemae vs honne is a journey they repeatedly fail
- They don't think their behavior is remarkable which makes it funnier

### The Classmate Perspective

- Nobody can categorize their relationship
- Skips all recognized relationship steps in Japanese dating culture
- Classmates who just started nervously dating feel personally attacked
- Everyone waits for drama that never comes

## Recurring Comedy Beats

### The Teacher Who Gave Up

- First week: constantly separating them
- Month two: just sighs
- Eventually seats them together because fighting it is more disruptive

### The Lunch Scene Running Gag

- She eats off his tray
- He drinks from her bottle
- Classmates watch like nature documentary observers
- Someone asks and they look confused

### The Unshakeable Couple

- Girls attempt to create jealousy that bounces off
- Boys try to befriend him expecting relationship complaints
- He genuinely has none
- Waiting for a breakup arc that never arrives

### Lost in Translation

- Directness causing social chaos
- Misreading Japanese social cues
- Accidentally breaking unspoken rules constantly

---

## The Serious Undercurrent

### Trauma Markers

- She flinches when someone mentions her mother's agency
- He has rough hands that don't match his background
- They instinctively close ranks when feeling threatened
- Survival instincts from their secondments surface unexpectedly

### The Emotional Core

- They chose each other at 13 knowing the cost
- Survived punishment designed to break them
- Their love isn't teenage butterflies it's forged through hardship
- A quiet moment: **"I'd choose Jeju again"**
- The question: is this love or survival bonding? Does it matter?

### The Bigger Picture

- They know they're chess pieces
- Japan may be the families' second attempt at controlled proximity
- Neutral territory where neither family dominates
- Possibly grooming them for Asia-Pacific operations
- The tension between genuine love and fulfilling a corporate plan they
  originally rejected

## Potential Chapter Concepts

Each episode explores one idea:

1. **First day** - arrival and immediate cultural shock
2. **The seating arrangement** - teacher tries to separate them
3. **The lunch scene** - food sharing scandal
4. **The confession culture** - classmates explain how dating works here
5. **The crush girl** - someone tries to approach the boy
6. **Lost in translation** - directness meets tatemae disaster
7. **The gossip investigation** - classmate tries to get their story
8. **The class rep intervention** - PDA crackdown attempt
9. **Academic struggles** - classical Japanese humbles them
10. **The rough hands** - someone notices and asks questions (serious episode)
11. **The jacket steal** - cold day casual intimacy breaks brains
12. **Pet names** - classmates hear them switch languages mid-conversation
13. **The mother's agency** - something triggers her flinch (serious episode)
14. **Sports festival** - competitive couple energy
15. **The new couple contrast** - classmates start dating and realize the gap
16. **The parents check in** - reminder of the chess game (serious episode)
