# The Lore Bible — Isabelle & Caspar

---

## Core Premise

> **Two people who never learned distance are placed in a world that requires
> it.**

The story follows Isabelle and Caspar—already a couple in all but
name—navigating the social structure of a Japanese public high school. Their
relationship is not developing; it is **already fully formed**, and the world
around them must react to it.

The narrative focuses on:

- **Social friction**
- **Cultural mismatch**
- **Observation vs. participation**

---

## Tone & Narrative Rules

### Emotional Language

- Feelings are expressed through **behavior, structure, and restraint**
- No overt dramatization or melodrama
- Intimacy is shown, never explained

---

### Conflict Boundaries (Locked)

This story does **not include**:

- Family political conflict
- Corporate intrigue
- Love triangles
- External antagonists

All tension arises from:

- Environment (school, society)
- Perception (how others interpret them)
- Adjustment (or refusal to adjust)

---

## Identity & Naming

**Isabelle van Rijn / Shin Ji-won (신지원)** **Caspar Waldstein / Ryu Tae-sung
(류태성)**

### Usage Rules

- School → European given names (“Isabelle”, “Caspar”)
- Formal/administrative → surnames (“van Rijn”, “Waldstein”)
- Private/intimate → selective use of Korean names (“Ji-won”, “Tae-sung”)

### Identity & Naming - Function

- European names → social default
- Korean names → **precise, intentional intimacy**
- Japanese honorifics used by others in school context

> Name usage reflects context, distance, and intent.

## The Setting

### Setagaya, Tokyo — Residential Base

- Quiet, upper-middle-class Tokyo ward
- Modern, minimal, fully managed home
- Feels **maintained**, not lived-in

---

### Japanese Public High School

- Governed by:
  - Group awareness
  - Implicit rules
  - Social restraint

### Their Position

> **Soft center of attention**

- Not trying to stand out
- Impossible to ignore
- Constantly observed, rarely confronted

- Childhood proximity engineered by families

- No negotiation

They function as:

> **An old married couple placed in a teenage environment**

### Key Traits

- No jealousy

- No performative affection

Their closeness is:

> **Habitual, not intentional**

- He adjusts without reacting
- They share space instinctively

### Constraints

- Never exaggerated
- Never explained

## The Secondment (Separation Period)

---

### Isabelle — Trainee System

- Environment:
  - surveillance
  - evaluation
  - performance control

- Developed:
  - technical perfection
  - emotional composure
  - ability to simulate intimacy

---

### VESPER (Pre-Debut Outcome)

- Isabelle selected for **final debut lineup**
- Removed immediately before debut

> Final notice: _“Your contract will not proceed.”_

- No failure
- No scandal
- Purely structural decision

---

### Aftermath

- Maintains **minimal, distant contact** with 1–2 members
- After debut:
  - communication becomes sparse
  - emotionally warm but limited

> The life she was part of continues without her.

---

### Caspar — Jeju Secondment

- Agricultural cooperative placement
- Physical labor, minimal speech
- Isolation and repetition

---

### Defining Memory

> **Working in silence during a storm**

- No instruction
- No pause
- Task continues regardless

---

### Result

- Emotionally contained
- Physically grounded
- Does not simulate behavior

---

## Core Contrast

- Isabelle → trained to perform
- Caspar → incapable of performance

> With each other, neither performs.

---

## The Reunion

- Occurs shortly before transfer
- No adjustment period

> They resume exactly where they left off.

## Household Structure

### Housekeeper

**Name:** Park Sun-hee (박선희) **Age:** Mid-40s

### Background

- Korean
- Previously within Shin family network
- Assigned through family office

---

### Role

- Maintains household stability
- Provides cultural continuity
- Quiet observer

---

### Routine

- Morning preparation before school
- Evening meals completed in advance
- Minimal overlap with emotional space

---

### Reporting

- Sends **broad, non-invasive summaries**:
  - general well-being
  - schedule consistency

- No behavioral detail

---

### Awareness

She notices:

- Their closeness
- Their synchronization
- Their lack of distance

She does not:

- Comment
- Interfere
- Correct

> Her role is stability, not control.

---

## Family Influence (Operational Summary)

The families are not active forces in the present timeline.

They function as:

> **Environmental architects and residual pressure systems**

---

### Structural Influence

- Designed:
  - relocation to Japan
  - public school placement
  - shared household

---

### Logic

- Separation failed → strengthened attachment
- Intervention replaced by:

> **Passive containment**

---

### Why Japan

- Neutral ground
- Socially restrictive
- Distant from all primary power bases

---

### Why Public School

- Not “normalcy,” but:
  - indirect social regulation
  - peer-enforced boundaries

---

### Why Cohabitation

> “If left undisturbed, it may stabilize.”

---

### Ongoing Presence

- Family office manages logistics invisibly
- Life remains structured, even without interference

---

### Behavioral Residue

**Isabelle:**

- Composed
- precise
- socially aware

**Caspar:**

- quiet
- non-reactive
- physically grounded

**Together:**

- no rebellion
- no performance

> They simply continue existing as they are.
