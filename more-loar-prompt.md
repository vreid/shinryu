# Refined Prompt

Write a **long, slow, observational scene** set in a Japanese public high school
classroom in Setagaya. Early morning after summer break. The room is initially
empty.

Caspar is seated. Isabelle is sitting on his lap. This is completely normal for
them—no discussion, no awareness, no explanation.

---

## Primary Focus — The Social Ecosystem

The scene is **not about Isabelle and Caspar.** They are the still center. The
scene is about **the room reacting to them.**

The narrative engine is:

- whispered two-line exchanges between classmates
- LINE messages shown as text (use 「」for Japanese, with translation)
- notes passed on torn paper
- internal micro-observations (a glance held one beat too long, a bag set down
  slowly)
- seat-choice psychology — who sits near them, who doesn't, and why
- the specific moment someone pulls out their phone under the desk

Each arriving student or group should **add a new reaction type.** Stack them:

| Arrival            | Reaction Layer                                         |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| First student      | Freeze-and-redirect. Pretends to check phone.          |
| Second pair        | Whispered exchange. Attempted categorization.          |
| Small group        | Active LINE thread. Speculation escalates.             |
| The quiet observer | Watches others watching. Says nothing.                 |
| Late arrival       | Gets briefed by seatmate via note. Reads it. Looks up. |

This is a suggested arc — not rigid. But the **variety and escalation of
reaction formats** is the point.

---

## Reaction Texture — Be Specific

Reactions must feel **Japanese high school.** That means:

- No one says anything directly
- Judgment travels through side-channels: LINE groups, whispers behind hands,
  eye contact between friends across the room
- Physical avoidance is a statement (choosing a far seat, angling a chair)
- Curiosity is disguised as disinterest
- Someone will definitely screenshot-text a friend in another class
- At least one student is genuinely unbothered and this should also be visible

Show the **grammar of social discomfort in a conformity-aware space.** The
disruption is quiet. The tension is structural.

---

## LINE Messages

When showing LINE chats, format them clearly:

> **Yuki:** 「あの二人何？」(What's with those two?) **Moe:**
> 「分からん。付き合ってるの？」(No idea. Are they dating?)

These should feel **dashed off** — short, clipped, half-formed thoughts. Not
exposition. Students reacting in real time.

---

## Behavior Rules — Main Pair

- Isabelle and Caspar are **inert**. They do not perform.
- They talk to each other occasionally — mundane, half-finished sentences
- Any physical adjustment is practical (shifting weight, reaching for a bag)
- They do not notice the room. The room notices them.

---

## Dialogue & Language Rules

- Dialogue should be **present but minimal and natural**
- **All non-English dialogue MUST include inline translation**

Format:

- 「大丈夫？」(Are you okay?)
- "괜찮아." (I'm fine.)

Language:

- Isabelle & Caspar → English baseline, occasional Korean
- Other students → Japanese

---

## Tone

- Observational, not editorial
- Controlled, socially literate
- No melodrama, no backstory, no exposition
- Uncomfortable in the way a too-quiet room is uncomfortable
- Let pauses do work

---

## Ending Beat

A classmate finally addresses them directly — politely, in Japanese. They
respond correctly. But something about the response **doesn't land right.** Not
rude. Not wrong. Just slightly outside the expected shape of the interaction.
The classmate turns back to their seat and the room stays exactly as tense as it
was.

---

## Goal

Build an entire scene where two people do **nothing unusual to themselves** and
the social fabric around them **quietly strains to accommodate it.** The reader
should feel the room more than the couple.
