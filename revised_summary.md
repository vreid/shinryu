#

## Before the Gate (Chapter 1)

Introduces Isabelle (Shin Ji-won) and Caspar (Ryu Tae-sung) on their first
morning at Seta-sō. Separately, they prepare for school under the watch of
Auntie Choi and Auntie Park — caregivers, not handlers, though Isabelle notices
Auntie Choi's evening call to Seoul lasts longer than a grocery list warrants.
Their walk follows the Tamagawa river toward Futako-Tamagawa Station. At the
shoe lockers, a silent, charged nod. They confirm via LINE that they are both
here. Internally, both register the same unresolvable question: if the parents
engineered this proximity, does walking toward each other mean autonomy or
compliance?

## Parallel Lines (Chapter 2)

The second day finds Caspar and Isabelle in adjacent homerooms, separated for
most blocks by the curriculum track system but aware of each other through walls
and stairwells. Seta-sō's no-chime policy means no bells mark the passages —
only their own internal clocks. Caspar befriends Kenji, Ren, and Daiki; Isabelle
connects with Yuki and Sora. They eat lunch separately but text in Korean, the
language they kept private at Le Parcours. During cleaning duty, a tense hallway
encounter. Isabelle tells him, "Not yet." After 2.5 years of enforced silence,
two words in Korean feel like breaking a seal.

## Anywhere (Chapter 3)

Caspar and Isabelle share their first real lunch on a bench behind the
gymnasium, trading strawberries and madeleines. They cautiously bridge the
2.5-year silence — school, the housekeepers, the terms of their new lives.
Caspar listens carefully as she describes her Setagaya routine, offering nothing
that reveals how much he already knows. He has known about Shin Entertainment
since the Alps. He tracked her through the 240p video on 15 minutes of daily
internet. But she hasn't told him, and he won't take that from her. That
evening, Isabelle bakes madeleines in her small kitchen; Caspar develops film
and studies the contact sheets with the same obsessive attention he once gave a
pixelated screen.

## The Weight of Small Things (Chapter 4)

Isabelle brings madeleines for her classmates; Yuki and Hina privately note the
professional precision — this is not hobby baking. Caspar's friend group
formalizes HCCL (Hallway Cleaning Crew Lunch) and a food-sharing tradition
anchored by whatever Auntie Park packed that morning. Isabelle and Caspar
schedule their first weekend outing — Saturday in Daikanyama. The Aunties
coordinate what Caspar privately calls the "diplomatic channel," warm and
efficient. But that evening, Isabelle hears Auntie Choi on the phone: names,
schedules, the phrase "adjusting well." She files it away without comment.

## Daikanyama (Chapter 5)

Saturday afternoon: their first real excursion beyond Setagaya, a Daikanyama
café. Caspar explains his mother Helena's design philosophy; Isabelle describes
Shin Hee-yeon's narrative-driven empire. Caspar finally explains why he turned
away on the terrace in Switzerland: "I saw the design. The whole thing — us, the
school, the friendship — it was built. I couldn't let them own that moment." Not
fear of what he felt. Refusal to be authored. Isabelle says the problem was
timing, not the kiss itself, though her internal register notes the distinction
— his reason is political, not romantic. He photographs her in the golden
afternoon light. This is their first real afternoon alone since Le Parcours.

## Exposure (Chapter 6)

Sunday: Caspar develops the Daikanyama roll and is struck by the color portraits
— Isabelle in warm light, composed and unguarded in a way the 240p video never
allowed. Monday's rain forces them to eat lunch on a fourth-floor stairwell,
where the no-chime silence holds like a room of its own. She feeds him a mandu;
they plan for Wednesday. Tuesday: Sora discovers Caspar is a VESPER fan. For
Caspar, this isn't a casual hobby being exposed — it's the perimeter of
something survival-deep, and his reaction is calibrated accordingly. The two
friend groups collide at the shoe lockers, pulled together by the gravity of
overlapping secrets.

## The Prints (Chapter 7)

Wednesday after school, they walk to a photo shop near Futako-Tamagawa to
collect the color Daikanyama prints. At Caspar's apartment, he shows her three
black-and-white frames he printed himself — her face, her profile, the empty
street after she left. The same afternoon, two readings: the color prints are
beautiful; the B&W prints are honest. She feels his heartbeat through his wrist;
he says, "Not yet." At home, she asks the VESPER group chat what to do when
liking someone is terrifying. Jiwoo replies: "Being scared means it's real."
Jiwoo — who knows her debut exists because Isabelle surrendered — means it
completely.

## Convergence (Chapter 8)

The friend groups merge into a single lunch table at Seta-sō. On Saturday,
Isabelle takes Caspar to Yanaka — uncurated, lived-in, the kind of neighborhood
that doesn't perform. He opens up about the 2.5 years at the Alpenhof:
twelve-hour shifts, former classmates from Le Parcours mocking him from the
lobby, finding purpose through the Leica. Walking home, they name the parents'
corporate merger plot out loud. The discomfort is explicit: choosing each other
and complying with the design are indistinguishable from the outside. They hold
hands anyway, unable to resolve it, unwilling to let the irresolution stop them.
That night, Caspar writes 여기서부터 in his notebook. _From here._

## Threshold (Chapter 9)

Monday morning, they walk arm-in-arm through the Seta-sō gate. The friend groups
react: Kenji celebrates, Daiki notes Caspar's increased "smile frequency," Sora
calls it a billboard-level declaration. After photography club, they stop at a
street corner near the school and share their first kiss. He asks her to be his
girlfriend over LINE that evening; she replies: "Wasn't I already?"

## Glass Hour (Chapter 10)

Daily couple life settles into the combined lunch table. Sora teaches Kenji
VESPER choreography; he's terrible and committed. Caspar nearly slips —
recalling a pre-debut formation detail that only someone who studied the
underground material would know. Isabelle covers smoothly, redirecting the
conversation, though a flicker of confusion crosses her face. She files it
alongside other small incongruities she can't yet name. Saturday by the Tama
River: she teaches him the new single's choreography. He performs the "Glass
Hour" chorus for her, clumsy and sincere. They kiss on the riverbank.

## Comeback (Chapter 11)

VESPER drops their second single, "Somebody Knows." Caspar listens at midnight
and his reaction is visceral — this music anchored him through the Alpenhof,
through the 15-minute internet windows, through the pixel-blurred file that
proved Isabelle was still somewhere in the world. Isabelle listens with the
weight of someone who trained alongside these voices and walked away. Sora drags
Isabelle to a Shibuya fan event; she attends in disguise but Yuna spots her from
the stage. The VESPER members text her afterward — gently confronting, carefully
protective.

## Somebody Knows (Chapter 12)

Saturday night in the VESPER group chat. Yuna, Minji, Haeun, and Jiwoo process
Isabelle's surprise appearance at the fan event. Jiwoo is hurt she didn't say
hello; Minji defends Isabelle's need for privacy; Yuna oscillates between
outrage and affection. Beneath all of it runs the knowledge none of them can set
down: their careers exist because Isabelle surrendered her freedom. The guilt
makes their protectiveness fierce and slightly uneven. They reaffirm their bond
and agree to guard her secret, understanding that protecting Isabelle is the one
debt they can partially repay.

## The Cost of Things (Chapter 13)

Ren quietly reveals his racing spikes are worn out, and his family can't afford
replacements before the prefectural meet. The group absorbs the information
without pity. Caspar, through the upcoming photography exhibition, anonymously
arranges for proceeds to cover carbon-fiber spikes — 38,000 JPY. Ren accepts
them in silence. The chapter orbits a truth Caspar and Isabelle once took for
granted: what things cost when you can't buy your way past them.

## Exhibition (Chapter 14)

The photography club mounts its hallway exhibition and charity sale at Seta-sō.
Caspar's Yanaka and Tokyo prints draw serious attention — his compositional eye,
honed by years of obsessive visual attention, reads as precocious talent to his
teachers and peers. Isabelle brings a tarte au citron for the group. Yuki runs a
meticulously organized sales table. The proceeds cover Ren's spikes and then
some. The event binds the group tighter and earns Caspar respect beyond the
club.

## The Last Frame (Chapter 15)

The full group attends the prefectural track meet at Komazawa Olympic Park,
bearing Kenji's hand-painted banner. Ren competes in the 800m and 1500m, buoyed
by the new spikes and the noise from the stands. Caspar photographs the event.
Frame 34: a blind shot, the Leica held behind him as he turns to kiss Isabelle's
cheek. Afterward, she tells him she'll be in Seoul for the first two weeks of
summer break. He tells her Munich. The separation is already drawing its shape.

## Long Distance (Chapter 16)

Mid-July: the last day of the first term. The expanded group shares a final
combined lunch, including Morimoto-sensei. Summer plans are exchanged. Isabelle
flies to Seoul, Caspar to Munich. The chapter traces their separation across
time zones via LINE messages and late-night calls, the distance rhyming with the
exile but different in one essential way — this time, they chose it, and this
time, they can speak.

## Homecoming (Chapter 17)

Isabelle arrives at Incheon and is ambushed at the barrier by Yuna, Minji, and
Jiwoo. The reunion is warm but striated — these four women owe their careers to
a deal Isabelle made under duress, and they know it. The teasing about her
boyfriend carries undercurrents of gratitude and guilt that surface in small
gestures: Jiwoo holds the madeleines a beat too long; Haeun won't quite meet her
eyes during the group photo. Isabelle returns to the practice room and dorm life
she left behind. For the first time, the space feels like somewhere she chose to
revisit, not somewhere she was held.

## Frame 36 (Chapter 18)

Caspar spends time in Munich, revisiting the Alpenhof and reconnecting with
Gerhard, Maggie, and Frau Lindner — the people Helena threatened to destroy as
leverage. He saves the last frame on his Komazawa roll for Seoul. At Incheon
arrivals, Isabelle is waiting at the barrier. He shoots Frame 36: her face, the
moment before she sees him. Helena Waldstein is in Seoul — not for a polite
introduction but for a diplomatic encounter disguised as dinner. Isabelle and
Helena face each other knowing exactly what the other represents. The
conversation is technically social, functionally corporate. Caspar watches every
microexpression. Both teenagers perform accordingly, and the meal ends with
nothing resolved except the confirmation that the parents are still watching.

## Two Weeks in Seoul (Chapter 19)

Two weeks in Seoul together. Isabelle guides Caspar through the geography of her
exile — backstreets behind Shin Entertainment, the convenience store for
midnight triangle kimbap, the overpass to the trainee dorms. Caspar photographs
compulsively, comparing Seoul's layered noise to Tokyo's restraint. On the fifth
day, walking through Apgujeong, he names a building she hasn't pointed out — the
vocal annex where lessons ran until 11 PM. She stops. She never told him about
the annex. The confession follows: the shared staff computer, the 15-minute
daily window, the fan forums, the 240p video of Jiwoo and a shadow trainee he
watched until the pixels burned into his retinas. She goes quiet for a long
time. His knowing feels like devotion and surveillance in the same breath — an
echo of the parental design she's spent years resisting. But the difference,
when she finds it, is that he never used the knowledge as leverage. He used it
to survive. By the trip's end, he gives her the developed print of Frame 34 —
the blind Komazawa kiss. 여기서부터, she says. _From here._ No more performed
ignorance.

## Fifty-Five Thousand (Chapter 20)

September 1st: the second term opens. Caspar and Isabelle reunite at the Seta-sō
gate. He gives her Frame 36 — her face at the Incheon barrier, now developed and
printed in his Setagaya darkroom. School life resumes: combined lunches,
photography club, the rhythms of the curriculum tracks pulling them apart during
the day and gathering them at the table by noon. Their relationship is fully
public, the group closer than before, and the Seoul honesty has left them
lighter. Something has shifted — not just between them, but in how Caspar
carries himself. The exhibition's success, the film work, the quiet reputation —
Seta-sō is beginning to feel like a place he built, not a place he was sent.

## The Shape of It (Chapter 21)

Late October. A magazine left in a classroom connects Shin Hee-yeon to Isabelle,
and by extension, to Shin Entertainment and VESPER. Sora discovers the truth
first — and as the group's fiercest VESPER fan, her reaction carries specific,
personal weight. Isabelle confirms everything to her friends. The revelation
ripples outward through the school. In the shoe-locker area, Kenji delivers a
spontaneous speech defending the group's unity: what they built at this table is
real, regardless of where anyone came from or what name is on a magazine cover.
The table chooses to stay. The chapter ends with the group intact and the full
shape of their bond — tested, strained, held — finally visible.

## Key structural additions

These are the three elements that didn't exist in the soft version. Flag
anything you want adjusted before I generate.

### 1. The Caspar-knows revelation (Chapter 19)

This is the biggest new beat. Isabelle spends months not knowing that Caspar has
tracked her since the Alps. His knowledge surfaces in Seoul when he names a
building she never described. The confession reframes every earlier moment where
he listened carefully and said nothing. It's the relationship's deepest internal
test and mirrors the parental surveillance without replicating it.

### 2. 여기서부터 as recurring motif (Chapters 8 and 19)

Written privately in Caspar's notebook in Chapter 8 when they first choose each
other despite the parents' design. Spoken aloud by Isabelle in Chapter 19 after
the confession clears the last layer of performed ignorance. Same phrase,
different weight — private intention becomes shared declaration.

### 3. The Aunties' reporting thread (Chapters 1, 4, and ambient)

Small, specific moments — a phone call that runs too long, names and schedules
mentioned in Korean, the phrase "adjusting well." Never confronted directly,
never fully resolved. The Aunties are genuinely warm and genuinely reporting.
Both things are true, and the characters live inside that ambiguity.
