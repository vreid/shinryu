# 📖 THE "Shin-Ryu" LORE BOOK

## 1. THE GRAND SCHEME (THE PARENTS' PLOT)

The European/Korean billionaire parents of Caspar and Isabelle have been
friendly for years. Recognizing a massive potential synergy between their
empires, they are quietly engineering a corporate marriage merger between their
children.

- **The Catalyst:** The kids were best friends at a Swiss boarding school, but a
  rejected kiss (due to timing) pulled them apart.
- **The Gap Year:** Instead of intervening directly, the parents sent them on
  separate, grueling gap-year training regimes to mature them.
- **The "Happy Accident":** They secretly manipulated their high school
  applications so both teens would surprisingly collide at **Tokyo Metropolitan
  Sakuragaoka High School**, a completely average public school. The goal: force
  them to rely on each other in the "real world" and fall in love naturally.

## 2. HOUSE VAN RIJN & SHIN (Isabelle’s Lineage)

**The Dutch Old Money: House van Rijn**

- **Father:** Willem van Rijn. Head of van Rijn Private Banking & Global Real
  Estate. Manages centuries-old European wealth, financing stadiums, luxury
  hotels, and global infrastructure.
- **Uncle:** Bastiaan van Rijn. CEO of the van Rijn European Media Conglomerate
  (broadcasting/streaming rights).

**The Korean Chaebol: The Shin Empire**

- **Mother:** Shin Hee-yeon. CEO of Shin Entertainment, Asia’s premier K-Pop
  Idol Agency.
- **Uncle:** Shin Dong-il. Chairman of Shin Telecom & Tech, the overarching
  conglomerate that gives Shin Ent its global reach.

### 👑 Isabelle van Rijn / Shin Ji-won (15)

- **Appearance:** 5’10", statuesque but natural. Heavy, wavy caramel-blonde
  hair, glowing pale "glass skin," large hazel-green upturned eyes. Has the
  flawless, effortless posture of a trained dancer.
- **The Gap Year (The Trainee):** She spent the year living and training in
  Seoul with **VESPER**, her mother’s trendiest, most famous 4-member girl
  group, right before their debut. She has idol-level stamina, dance, and vocal
  skills, but views these untouchable mega-stars as annoying older sisters who
  text her memes and steal her snacks.
- **Personality:** Calm, observant, and graceful. Uses a "language filter"
  (pretending not to understand fluent Japanese) to weed out fake friends.
- **Hobbies/Skills:** High-End European Patisserie. She bakes flawlessly using
  her apartment's imported Miele oven, bringing Michelin-level pastries to
  school in cheap Tupperware. She is teaching Caspar Dutch (her German is
  terrible).
- **Tokyo Setup:** Lives in a quiet, high-end apartment in Minami-Aoyama. Looked
  after by "Auntie Choi" (strict Korean housekeeper) and a Japanese male tutor
  who acts as her legal guardian. Works part-time as a PA/Translator at Shin
  Ent's Tokyo branch (where she casually fetches coffee for VESPER).

## 3. HOUSE WALDSTEIN & RYU (Caspar’s Lineage)

**The German Old Money: House Waldstein**

- **Mother:** Helena Waldstein. Founder/CEO of Waldstein Botanicals &
  Aesthetics, the #1 ultra-luxury cosmetics and skincare brand in Asia (highly
  coveted by idols).
- **Uncle:** Klaus Waldstein. Manages the traditional Bavarian wealth, estates,
  and family trusts.

**The Korean Chaebol: The Ryu Empire**

- **Father:** Ryu Ji-seok. Chairman of Ryu BioPharma & Chemical (cutting-edge
  medical and laboratory research).
- **Aunt:** Ryu Myung-hee. Chairwoman of Ryu Retail, running all the luxury
  department stores, duty-free networks, and high-end malls in Asia.

### 📷 Caspar Waldstein / Ryu Tae-sung (15)

- **Appearance:** 6’1", lean, broad-shouldered. Striking aristocratic features,
  flawless pale skin, thick straight dark ash-blonde hair, icy pale blue
  almond-shaped eyes.
- **The Gap Year (The Porter):** His Uncle Klaus forced him to work incognito at
  a brutal, traditional 5-star hotel in the Bavarian Alps as a bottom-tier
  bellboy/porter. Hauling heavy luggage for 12 hours a day gave him a dense,
  muscular core and broad shoulders. Dealing with screaming, entitled rich
  people gave him a dead-eyed, stoic poker face and a deep hatred for flashy
  wealth.
- **Personality:** Stoic resting ice-face, brutally direct, fiercely protective.
  Also uses the "language filter" to avoid arrogant peers.
- **Hobbies/Skills:** Analog Film Photography. He shoots on a vintage $10,000
  35mm Leica and develops the film in his apartment darkroom. Isabelle is his
  secret muse. He is also a massive, secret **VESPER** fanboy—he collects their
  photocards and deadpans their point-choreography flawlessly. Teaches Isabelle
  German (his Dutch is terrible).
- **Tokyo Setup:** Lives in a sleek apartment in Hiroo. Looked after by "Auntie
  Park" (fussy Korean housekeeper who cooks amazing meals) and a Japanese male
  tutor. Works part-time doing heavy-lifting stockroom inventory at Ryu Retail
  in Ginza.

## 4. THE TOKYO DYNAMIC

**The "Stealth Wealth" Rules** They do not flex. To them, massive wealth is just
frictionless utility. Slip-ups include:

- Casual $150 international shipping for gummy bears.
- Wearing unbranded $4,000 Loro Piana cashmere sweaters.
- The "tutor" picking them up in a debadged, bulletproof AMG E-Class.
- Eating $200/pound A5 Wagyu at home because it’s "retail surplus."

**The Romance** They are an absolute united front. Bypassing Japanese teenage
awkwardness, they engage in casual European PDA (kisses at the school gates,
holding hands), deeply confusing their peers. They speak Korean privately in
public as their secret love language.

**The Friends ("The Adopters")** Six normal kids who befriended them before
knowing they were rich. Caspar and Isabelle are fiercely loyal to them.
Eventually, all friends get part-time jobs at the teens' family empires without
realizing who the real bosses are.

- _Isabelle's Girls:_ Yuki (Organized mom-friend), Sora (Trend-chaser), Hina
  (Quiet observer/smart one).
- _Caspar's Boys:_ Kenji (Loudmouth class clown), Ren (Athletic pragmatist),
  Daiki (Tech/gamer nerd).
