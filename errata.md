### 1. Hayashi-san glasses assigned to wrong person

**Line ~445:** Caspar's Hayashi-san is described with "rectangular glasses."

Hayashi-san. Caspar's has no glasses.  
**Fix:** Remove "rectangular glasses" from Caspar's Hayashi-san; add it to
Isabelle's Hayashi-san description (line ~553).

### 2. Doenjang-jjigae made by wrong auntie

**Line ~163:** Auntie Choi makes doenjang-jjigae for Isabelle's breakfast.  
**Lore food canon (§8):** Doenjang-jjigae is Auntie Park's dish.  
**Fix:** Either change the cook to Auntie Park, or have Auntie Choi make a
different stew (e.g., kimchi-jjigae).

### 3. Terrace messages displayed in English only

**Lines ~506–510:** Isabelle's three terrace messages appear in English without
Korean text.  
**Context:** All other intimate LINE exchanges use Korean with inline English
translation.  
**Note:** Arguably intentional — they were at a Swiss boarding school and may
have written in English. Flagging for consistency.

## Chapter 4

### 7. Korean language error — Jiwoo calls Isabelle "언니"

**Lines ~726, ~757–758:** Jiwoo (age 18–19) calls Isabelle (age 15) "언니"
(unnie = older sister).  
**Fact:** In Korean, "언니" is used by females to address **older** females.
Jiwoo is 3–4 years older than Isabelle. She should address Isabelle by name
(e.g., "지원아") or drop the address term. Isabelle would call Jiwoo "언니," not
the reverse.  
**Fix:** Replace "언니" with "지원아" (casual address to younger) or just "지원"
throughout Jiwoo's dialogue.  
**Note:** This error recurs in Ch7 and Ch12 (see entries #15 and #20).

### 8. Club recruitment timing contradicts Ch1

**Line ~489:** "Friday afternoon. Club recruitment." (Occurs same week.)  
**Ch1, line ~921:** Ōtsuka-sensei says club recruitment begins "next week."  
**Fix:** Either change Ch1 to say "this week" or move the Ch4 club fair to the
following week.

### 9. POV pattern break — two POVs in one section

**Section IV (~lines 488–692):** Begins in Isabelle's POV, shifts to Caspar's at
~line 582 with no `---` break.  
**Lore:** "Sections alternate between the two protagonists."  
**Fix:** Insert a `---` break at the POV shift point.

## Chapter 6

### 11. Portra 400 developed in black-and-white chemistry

**Section I, ~line 26:** "Developer: Kodak HC-110, dilution B"  
**Fact:** HC-110 is a B&W developer. Portra 400 is a C-41 color negative film.
Developing it in HC-110 would not produce viable color negatives — it would
yield flat, monochrome-only images with no dye layers. The Ch7 lab technician
then describes "textbook Portra rendering" with "faithful skin tones," which is
impossible from HC-110 processing.  
**Fix:** Either change the film to Tri-X 400 (B&W) for this roll, or change the
developer to a C-41 kit, or have Caspar send the roll to a professional lab for
C-41 processing.

### 12. Wrong person credited for spotting VESPERspace app

**Section IV, ~lines 660–670:** Aoi (friend-of-a-friend in Class 1-2) spotted
the VESPERspace app on Caspar's phone.  
**Lore (§6, Daiki's profile):** "Spotted the VESPER fan app on Caspar's phone —
the intel that started the 'is Ryu a fan?' thread."  
**Fix:** Reconcile — either the lore should credit Aoi as the original spotter
with Daiki as the relay, or the chapter should credit Daiki directly.

### 13. Wrong surname when addressing Auntie Choi

**Section V, ~line 1014:** Auntie Park says "Park-ssi" on the phone with Auntie
Choi.  
**Fact:** "Park-ssi" is Auntie Park's **own** surname. She is speaking to Choi
Eunji.  
**Fix:** "Choi-ssi."
