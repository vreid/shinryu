## Lyria 3 Pro Prompt

> K-pop comeback single by a four-member girl group. Mid-tempo contemporary
> R&B-pop, ~118 BPM. More mature and individual than the debut — each voice has
> its own movement before they braid together. Opens with ambient pads and a
> single vocal line, intimate and close-miked. Production is crisp and layered —
> electronic textures woven with warm bass, clean percussion, reverb that feels
> like a large empty room. Pre-chorus builds tension with stacked harmonies
> climbing in thirds, individual vocal lines weaving toward a shared resolve.
> Chorus opens wide — full arrangement, four-part harmonies in counterpoint, the
> four voices braiding and separating and braiding again, an insistent hook:
> "Somebody knows." The choreography is less synchronized than the debut —
> members have separate movement phrases that converge into unified formations,
> then split apart — the music should reflect this: moments of individuality
> inside cohesion. Second verse is stripped down, one voice over a sparse beat.
> Bridge features a low, soulful vocal dropping into the mix like a stone
> sinking into water — the last note carries a breath at the edge of the voice,
> the sound of someone singing and also letting go. A turn sequence: controlled
> rotation into a soft descent to floor level, like water. Final chorus explodes
> — ad-libs, countermelodies, the low voice rising to join the braid, four
> voices becoming one before the final cut. Korean and English lyrics. Female
> vocals. Sophisticated idol pop production. The song is about being seen —
> about someone knowing you, the terror and relief of recognition.

## Notes

- **"Somebody Knows"** — the title is about recognition: being seen, being
  known. It echoes Isabelle's secret (someone will find out) and Caspar's relief
  (someone knows him as himself, not the chaebol mask). It's also the terror and
  comfort VESPER feels: the public _knows_ them, but someone _really_ knows
  them.
- **Individual vocal lines** — vs. Glass Hour's synchronized choreo, this song
  gives each member her own verse/phrase, matching the story's description of
  "separate movement phrases" that converge. Minji (sharp, precise), Haeun
  (warm), Yuna (low, languid), Jiwoo (quiet, still).
- **Yuna's bridge** — "like a stone sinking into water" (Ch11:18). "깊이 더 깊이
  가라앉아" mirrors this. The third take's "breath at the edge of the voice, the
  sound of someone singing and also letting go" lands on "넌 날 알아" — the
  exhale after confession.
- **"We find each other in the dark"** — the English hook, repeated twice in the
  chorus per the AI's generation. Maps to the showcase venue: blue and white
  stage lights cutting through the dark, 200 people finding VESPER. Also maps to
  Caspar finding the song in a corridor in the worst year of his life.
- **"I see you even when I close my eyes / You see me even when I don't speak"**
  — Jiwoo, the quietest member, the one whose presence is felt not heard. Also
  maps to Isabelle and Caspar: they see each other's real selves without
  speaking.
- **"흩어지지 않아 이 마음을"** — "This heart won't scatter." The AI changed
  맘은 → 마음을 (shortened "heart" to the full word, and shifted the particle
  from topic to object). The choreography converges and separates; this line
  says the feeling holds even when the formation breaks.
- **"거짓말은 다 알고 있으니까"** — AI compressed the prompt's "거짓말은 하지
  않아도 돼 / 넌 이미 다 알고 있으니까" into one line: "You already know all the
  lies." Tighter, more direct.
- **"침묵 속에서도 들리니"** — AI changed 들리는 → 들리니 ("Can you hear it"
  instead of "I can hear it"). A question now — asking the listener, not
  stating. More intimate.
- **"Nothing left to hide with"** — the AI changed the prompt's "Nothing left to
  hide / When somebody knows" into a single line, replacing "when" with "with."
  Slightly different shade: not "when someone knows," but "nothing left to hide
  _with_ somebody knows" — hiding alongside knowledge, knowledge as companion
  rather than condition.
- **Chorus "Oh" + doubled "We find each other in the dark"** — the AI added
  emotional weight: repeating the hook and inserting a breath-like "Oh" before
  the final "Somebody knows." The repetition turns the line from statement into
  mantra.
- **Final Chorus dropped "숨겨왔던 너를"** — the AI removed the "hidden you"
  line, leaving only "너의 모습을 다" and "흩어지지 않아." The effect: by the
  final chorus, the hidden self has already been seen — the song no longer needs
  to name it.
- **"You know me, Somebody knows"** — paired as a single phrase in the final
  chorus. Not two separate statements but one: you-know-me and somebody-knows
  fused, the personal and the universal collapsing into each other.
- **~118 BPM** — slightly slower than Glass Hour's ~122 BPM. "More mature"
  (Ch11:213). The slower tempo allows the individual vocal phrases more room,
  matching the "separate movement phrases" choreography.
- **Library concept** — the MV is set in a dark wood-paneled library. The song's
  ambience — "like a large empty room" — echoes that space.
