# Chapter Summaries

## Before the Gate (Chapter 1)

Introduces Isabelle (Shin Ji-won) and Caspar (Ryu Tae-sung) on their first
morning at Sakuragaoka High. Separately, they prepare for school under the watch
of their housekeepers, Auntie Choi and Auntie Park, revealing their wealthy,
international backgrounds. They meet at the shoe lockers, exchange a silent,
charged nod, and confirm via LINE that they are both here.

## Parallel Lines (Chapter 2)

The second day finds Caspar and Isabelle in adjacent homerooms, quietly mapping
each other’s presence while meeting new classmates—Caspar befriends Kenji, Ren,
and Daiki; Isabelle connects with Yuki and Sora. They eat lunch separately but
text in Korean, culminating in a brief, tense hallway encounter during cleaning
duty where Isabelle tells him, "Not yet."

## Anywhere (Chapter 3)

Caspar and Isabelle share their first real lunch together on a bench behind the
gymnasium, trading strawberries and madeleines. They cautiously bridge the
year-long silence, discussing school, housekeepers, and the terms of their new
proximity. That evening Isabelle bakes madeleines for him, while Caspa develops
film and begins to suspect Isabelle’s hidden connection to VESPER.

## The Weight of Small Things (Chapter 4)

Isabelle brings madeleines for her classmates; Yuki and Hina privately note her
professional skill. Caspar’s friend group formalizes "HCCL" (Hallway Cleaning
Crew Lunch) and a food-sharing tradition. Isabelle and Caspar schedule their
first weekend meeting—Saturday in Daikanyama—while their housekeepers openly
coordinate the "diplomatic channel."

## Daikanyama (Chapter 5)

Their Saturday afternoon date at a Daikanyama café. Caspar explains his mother’s
design philosophy and Isabelle describes her mother’s narrative-driven empire.
Caspar finally reveals why he turned away from her kiss in Switzerland: he
wanted it too much and was terrified. Isabelle says the problem was timing. He
photographs her in the golden afternoon light.

## Exposure (Chapter 6)

Sunday: Caspar develops the Daikanyama roll and is struck by the color portraits
of Isabelle. Monday’s rain forces them to eat lunch on a fourth-floor stairwell,
where she feeds him a mandu and they plan for Wednesday: print pickup and his
apartment. Tuesday: Sora discovers Caspar is secretly a VESPER fan, and the two
friend groups collide at the shoe lockers.

## The Prints (Chapter 7)

Wednesday after school, they walk to Ebisu to collect the prints. In Caspar’s
darkroom, he shows her three frames: her face, her profile, and the empty street
after she left. She feels his racing heartbeat; he says, "Not yet." She takes
the prints home and asks her VESPER group chat what to do when liking someone is
scary; Jiwoo replies, "Being scared means it’s real."

## Convergence (Chapter 8)

The friend groups merge into a single lunch table. On Saturday, Isabelle takes
Caspar to Yanaka—an uncurated, lived-in neighborhood where he opens up about his
hotel year and discovering VESPER. Walking home through Minami-Aoyama, they
acknowledge their parents’ corporate merger plot, then consciously choose each
other anyway. They hold hands for the first time.

## Threshold (Chapter 9)

Monday morning, they walk arm-in-arm through the school gate, making their
relationship public. The friend groups react: Kenji celebrates, Daiki notes
Caspar’s increased "smile frequency," Sora dubs it a billboard-level
declaration. After photography club, they stop at a street corner and share
their first kiss. Caspar asks her to be his girlfriend over LINE; she replies,
"Wasn’t I already?"

## Glass Hour (Chapter 10)

Daily life as a couple settles in. The combined lunch table becomes a fixture,
and Sora begins teaching Kenji VESPER choreography. Caspar nearly reveals his
hidden VESPER expertise but Isabelle covers. On Saturday by the Tama River, she
teaches him the new single’s choreography, and he performs the "Glass Hour"
chorus for her. They kiss again.

## Comeback (Chapter 11)

VESPER drops their second single, "Somebody Knows." Isabelle and Caspar both
listen at midnight and share reactions. Sora drags Isabelle to a Shibuya fan
event; Isabelle attends in disguise but is spotted by Yuna from the stage. The
VESPER members later text Isabelle, confronting her gently about the surprise
appearance.

## Somebody Knows (Chapter 12)

Saturday night in the VESPER group chat. Yuna, Minji, Haeun, and Jiwoo process
Isabelle’s secret attendance at the event. Jiwoo is hurt she didn’t say hello;
Minji defends Isabelle’s need for privacy; Yuna oscillates between outrage and
affection. They ultimately reaffirm their bond and agree to protect her secret.

## The Cost of Things (Chapter 13)

Ren quietly reveals his racing spikes are worn out and his family can’t afford
new ones for the prefectural meet. The group absorbs the weight of his situation
without pity. Caspar, through the upcoming photography exhibition, anonymously
arranges for Ren to receive new carbon fiber spikes as a "fundraiser" sale. Ren
accepts them in silence.

## Exhibition (Chapter 14)

The photography club mounts its hallway exhibition and charity sale. Caspar’s
Yanaka and Tokyo prints draw serious attention. Isabelle brings a tarte au
citron for the group. Yuki runs a meticulously organized sales table. The
proceeds cover Ren’s spikes and more. The event binds the group closer, and
Caspar’s work earns respect beyond the club.

## The Last Frame (Chapter 15)

The whole group attends the prefectural meet at Komazawa Olympic Park, bearing a
hand-painted banner by Kenji. Ren competes in the 800m and 1500m, buoyed by
their support and his new spikes. Caspar photographs the event and takes a blind
"selfie" with Isabelle—Frame 34. She then tells him she will be in Seoul for the
first two weeks of summer break.

## Long Distance (Chapter 16)

The last day of the first term in mid-July. The expanded group shares a final
combined lunch, including Morimoto-sensei. Summer plans are exchanged. Caspar
and Isabelle part ways—she flies to Seoul, he to Munich. They promise to stay in
touch, and the chapter traces their separation across time zones via texts and
calls.

## Homecoming (Chapter 17)

Isabelle arrives at Incheon Airport and is ambushed at the barrier by Yuna,
Minji, and Jiwoo. She reunites with all four VESPER members at Shin
Entertainment, returning to the practice room and dorm life she left behind. She
gives Jiwoo madeleines, shares photos of Caspar, and is teased mercifully but
lovingly by the group for having a boyfriend.

## Frame 36 (Chapter 18)

Caspar spends time in Munich with his parents and revisits the Alpenhof hotel,
reconnecting with Maggie, Gerhard, and Frau Lindner. He saves the last frame on
his Komazawa roll—Frame 36—for Seoul. He flies to Incheon, where Isabelle is
waiting at the arrivals barrier. They kiss; Helena Waldstein formally meets
Isabelle for the first time.

## Two Weeks in Seoul (Chapter 19)

Caspar and Isabelle spend two weeks together in Seoul. She guides him through
the trainee-era neighborhoods behind Shin Entertainment, Gangnam backstreets,
and Cheonggyecheon Stream. Caspar photographs obsessively, comparing Seoul’s
noisy simultaneity to Tokyo’s restrained silence. He gives her Frame 34 (the
Komazawa kiss photo) and Frame 36 (her waiting at the barrier).

## Fifty-Five Thousand (Chapter 20)

September 1st: the second term begins. Caspar and Isabelle reunite at the school
gate. He hands her the developed prints. School life resumes—combined lunches,
photography club, the rhythms of class—now with their relationship fully public
and the group closer than before.

## The Shape of It (Chapter 21)

In late October, a magazine left in the classroom reveals Shin Hee-yeon is
Isabelle’s mother and that she is connected to Shin Entertainment. Sora
discovers the truth; Isabelle confirms it to her friends. The revelation ripples
through the school. Kenji delivers a spontaneous speech in the shoe-locker area
defending the group’s unity, and the chapter ends with the table choosing to
accept her, secrets and all.
